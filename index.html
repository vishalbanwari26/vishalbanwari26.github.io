Background Software Engineer → AI Researcher

I do my MSc in Computer Science at RPTU Kaiserslautern, specializing in Intelligent Systems. Currently working as an AI Research Intern at ABB, where I apply LLMs to industrial fault diagnosis and build RAG pipelines for log intelligence. Before pivoting to research in 2024, I spent seven years shipping software in C++/C# and Angular across industrial sectors, which gave me a strong sense for what AI actually needs to work in the real world.

My research sits at the intersection of human-in-the-loop learning, large language models, and AI sustainability. I care about the gap between what AI can do and how humans actually work with it. My published work covers the energy footprint of LLM APIs and web agents, and a framework called HILL that lets humans directly reshape how a model learns during training.
