### 📄 Publications

**This Is Taking Too Long — Investigating Time as a Proxy for Energy Consumption of LLMs**
*PerCom · 2026*
[arXiv:2603.15699](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15699)

Investigates inference time measurements as a proxy to approximate the energy costs of API-based LLMs, comparing estimations with actual measurements from locally hosted equivalents. Shows that time measurements allow inference of GPU models for API-based LLMs, enabling grounded energy cost estimations.

> ⚡ **Highlights:** Inference time as energy proxy · GPU model inference · API-based LLM cost transparency

---

**Promoting Sustainable Web Agents: Benchmarking and Estimating Energy Consumption through Empirical and Theoretical Analysis**
*AAAI · 2025*
[arXiv:2511.04481](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.04481)

Explores energy and CO₂ costs of web agents (e.g. OpenAI Operator, Google Mariner) empirically and theoretically. Shows that different agent design philosophies severely impact energy use, and that more energy ≠ better results.

> 🌿 **Highlights:** Web agent sustainability · Energy & CO₂ benchmarking · Transparency in agent design

---

**Human in the Latent Loop (HILL): Interactively Guiding Model Training Through Human Intuition**
*HHAI · May 2025*
[arXiv:2505.06325](https://www.arxiv.org/pdf/2505.06325)

Presents **HILL**, an interactive framework allowing users to incorporate human intuition into the model training loop by reshaping latent space representations via a distillation-inspired approach.

> 🧠 **Highlights:** Interactive latent guidance · Distillation from user-shaped space · Improved generalization under human influence
